The beach

Joss Bay is a golden sandy beach stretching over 200 m surrounded by chalky cliffs. The water is crystal blue with an easy entrance and there is also some good rock-pooling when the tide is low.

Water quality is good
It can get pretty busy during seasonal peaks
Life guards are on duty

Activities

A well established surf school on the beach hires boards and wet suits so you can take to the waves.

Kiddies corner with trampolines and bouncy castles is on hand to keep the younger ones entertained.

There is also a shop which sell all your beach needs: buckets, spades, kites, etc.

Refreshments

Small beach café selling chips, sandwiches and the like. Great cups of tea and lovely staff.

Parking

Parking is about £7 for the day in the official car park, although you can park for free on the long road opposite the beach (the one running alongside the field). This gets pretty busy in peak season though. There is disabled parking available in the car park.

Dogs

Dogs are not allowed on the beach in peak season (May - Oct) between 10am and 6pm

Toilets

There are toilets near to the beach (at the top of the slope, next to the car park) and a small shower. These are normally pretty clean. The loos are only open during peak season.

Access

Although advertised as easy access mummys gin fund reports that “the beach is reached via a steep slope. It is advertised as being accessible to people with a disability but you will need very strong arms to push an adult up the slope!”
